[On Freedom]{.underline}

Freedom has never meant indulgence to me. It is authorship. It is
alignment. It is binding myself to values I've freely chosen, even when
doing the opposite would feel easier in the moment.

Freedom requires more of you, not less. It isn't peace. It is total war
-- combat with the parts of yourself that want to keep you small and
broken. Every time you choose the path of least resistance, you give
away authorship and part of yourself. You become a passenger in your own
life, steered by fear, doubt, anxiety, or someone else's script. And the
truth is, what doesn't grow decays - a truth in nature, in the universe,
and in us.

So freedom is choosing the terms of engagement with the pain required
for growth. It is choosing steadiness, even confrontation, when retreat
would be easier. Because indulged enough, retreat and refusal to
commit - to a course of action, to yourself, to others, to your chosen
purpose - becomes a choice to commit to the silence of the grave
instead.

The test isn't whether I call myself free. It's whether my motion
matches my intent. Whether I choose the harder path that aligns with who
I am, instead of drifting into indulgence that numbs me.

That's the difference between free as in beer, and free as in liberty.

I don't picture ease when I think of freedom. I picture the pain that
comes with living authentically; not as punishment, but as proof I am
the one writing my story. Painful things refine you.

This is not a call for stoicism. It's a call to stop avoiding. To
embrace the harder path and the emotions that come with it: doubt,
exhaustion, anxiety, frustration, even fear. Strength is making space
for them without letting them take the wheel. Liberty is letting your
real self lead anyway.

I'm not claiming mastery. I'm claiming commitment. Every time I choose
that which is harder but true, I stand taller because of it.

Insight like this might be free as in beer. Living it never will be.
That's the challenge -- and the reward.

[On Forward Motion]{.underline}

For the first time in a while, I'm proud of the man I'm becoming.

The moments of our lives are punctuated by marks indelible. Some are
etched into the corners of our eyes or worn on our skin, some on our
hearts, others hidden in the quiet recesses of our mind. We can
acknowledge them or pretend they don't exist - but we can't unring a
bell.

Who we are is shaped not by what those moments do to us, but by what we
do with them. Wisdom is learning the differences: between values and
opinions, fact and fiction, what matters and what doesn't, who aligns
and who doesn't, and in hardship, standing tall or collapsing.

And yes - wisdom is also knowing when to swim with the current, when to
hold space for ambiguity, and when to hold fixed like a rock. Depth and
complexity aren't flaws; they're the point.

It's not about being better because of another birthday. It's about
leaning into the process, charging headlong into meaning, creating
meaning where there is none, and matching motion to intention. Doing the
hard thing while staying true to the man I am and the man I want to be.
Trusting that the ones who choose me today will choose me again
tomorrow. And trusting myself to know when it's time to love myself in
equal measure and deliver the kindness of a loving goodbye to those who
won't. I suppose my key insight is this: loving others well means loving
yourself well too.

Amor fati.

No looking back. No looking sideways. Only forward, with hard-won
experience, a full heart, and a wry half-smile.
